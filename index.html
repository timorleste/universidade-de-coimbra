Our mission is to help facilitate the newly independent Timor-Leste in accessing the voices and the key events in its past and fostering an understanding of its unique cultural heritage; to help unite and secure the identity of the country and its people; and to contribute to the forging of a new democratic nation. To ensure the survival of unique resources which contain and communicate the experience and the story of Timor-Leste and to place these at the service of peace and understanding, in Timor-Leste, Regionally and Internationally

Access to the Public Archive of Max Stahl Audiovisual Centre for Timor-Leste (CAMSTL) here. According to Unesco Timor-Leste is the first nation in history to achieve its independence through the power of audiovisual images reaching out to the international community. These images of popular struggle, bravery and peaceful commitment inspired people around the world to share in an international intervention to foster a new nation. They are a precious inheritance to the Timorese. The partnership between the Centro Audiovisual Max Stahl Timor-Leste (CAMSTL), the National University of East Timor (UNTL) and the University of Coimbra (UC) aims at the preservation and enrichment of the Max Stahl archive, so that it can be part of new research and educational projects at both universities and their academic partners. The CAMSTL on-line archive contains over 5000 hours of video material and grows around 400 hours each year. It includes historical images that changed the destiny of a nation in a collection inscribed by UNESCO in the Register of The Memory of the World in 2013 More on the CAMSTL collection. Typology of projects and activities: 1· Research and enrichment of the archive The assets of CAMSTL are the result of 25 years of work by Max Stahl and his co-workers, most of them Timorese, in filming and reporting for more than 12 years the period following the restauration of independence in East Timor, between 2003 and 2016. Due to the particular conditions under which this work developed much still needs to be done at the level of meta-data, identification of people, places and events, many explanations of the material need to be developed. This constitutes a long term process, that requires the contribution of expertise and points of view that were lacking at the time of filming and initial documentation. By its nature it is a collaborative effort that creates opportunities for students, researchers and professors to participate in an endeavor that will bare fruits for the future generations of Timorese and researchers all over the world. Enlarging the current partnership is welcomed in this context. 2· Partnerships for Education The CAMSTL archive can support the production of thematic collections of images and narratives for educational purposes, which can be used in a variety of settings and levels. Partnerships with educational institutions for this purpose are welcome. 3· Training and development of good practices in audiovisual archives The archive provides a rich training ground for the development of a variety of skills and competences which many different study programs could profit from. Mobility of staff and students, workshops and training in archives, media studies, visual historical sources are just a few of the many activities that can be developed around the CAMSTL archive. 4· Documentary and film production, support for open science projects. The CAMSTL archive includes material relevant for public oriented production of content in the areas of History, Anthropology, Social Sciences, Development Studies, Urban Planning, Politics, International Relations, Geography, Environmental Studies, Religious Studies, Music and Health. Both media companies and researchers looking for material to increase impact of their research are welcome to contact the CAMSTL archives. How to get involved CAMSTL welcomes enquiries for partnerships and access to the archive for individual researchers. Other than the history of the birth of East Timor as a nation the collection contains relevant material for a wide range of topics of interest for researchers and educational institutions.
